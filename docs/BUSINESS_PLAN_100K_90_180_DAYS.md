# AgentPress Business Plan: $100k+ Annualized Revenue in 90-180 Days

## Executive summary

The fastest path to earning more than $100k annually with AgentPress is not to launch it first as a self-serve SaaS. The fastest path is to use it as the operating system for a productized content service, sell a high-value monthly outcome to a narrow customer segment, and convert the repeatable parts into templates, case studies, and eventually software licensing.

The target is **$8,334+ monthly recurring revenue**, which equals **$100,008 annualized revenue**. A realistic near-term revenue mix:

| Offer | Price | Units needed | Monthly revenue |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Managed content engine | $2,000/mo | 4 clients | $8,000 |
| Setup/audit fee | $2,500 one-time | 2/mo | $5,000 |
| Template/license add-on | $299-$799/mo | 2-5 customers | $598-$3,995 |

Best 90-180 day target: **4-6 managed clients at $2k-$4k/month**, plus setup fees. That is more achievable than finding hundreds of low-price SaaS subscribers quickly.

This plan is not a guarantee of income. It is a focused execution plan for reaching a $100k+ annualized run rate by using AgentPress as a delivery advantage.

## How to use AgentPress operationally

### Client account structure

Create one AgentPress account per client or owned site.

For each account:

- Add the WordPress destination.
- Add research feeds.
- Create 1-3 pipelines.
- Store brand voice, audience, geography, and compliance rules as variables.
- Save all runs for audit history.

### Recommended pipelines

#### 1. Research-backed blog post

Agents:

1. Source analyst: summarizes selected feed inputs.
2. Angle strategist: finds the best POV for the client's audience.
3. Outline builder: creates SEO-friendly structure.
4. Body writer: writes the draft.
5. Editor: tightens tone, removes fluff, checks claims.
6. Title/excerpt writer: creates publish-ready metadata.
7. Image prompt writer: creates thumbnail or inline image prompt.

Use for weekly blog content.

#### 2. Local SEO service page support article

Agents:

1. Search intent analyst.
2. Local relevance mapper.
3. FAQ builder.
4. Body writer.
5. CTA editor.
6. Title/excerpt writer.

Use for local service businesses.

#### 3. Thought leadership recap

Agents:

1. Feed summarizer.
2. Trend extractor.
3. Contrarian POV builder.
4. LinkedIn/blog writer.
5. Editor.

Use for consultants and B2B experts.

#### 4. Newsletter-to-blog repurposer

Agents:

1. Source digest.

Use when clients already have newsletters or videos.

## Quality control rules

Do not let the platform publish fully unattended for paid clients at first. Use it to produce speed, consistency, and traceability, then add human judgment.

QA checklist:

- Is the claim supported by supplied source material or common evergreen knowledge?
- Does the article match the client's service area, audience, and offer?
- Is the title specific rather than generic?
- Is there a clear CTA?
- Are regulated topics reviewed with extra care?
- Are internal links added where relevant?
- Is the WordPress draft formatted cleanly?

Avoid regulated niches at first unless you can review carefully:

- Medical advice.
- Legal advice.
- Investment recommendations.
- Insurance claims.
- Tax advice.

Financial, legal, and health clients can still be good customers, but position the service as drafting and content operations, not professional advice.
